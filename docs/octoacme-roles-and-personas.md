# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Test Engineers

### Role Summary
QA/Test Engineers validate the quality and correctness of software before it reaches production. They design and execute test plans, identify defects, and work closely with developers to ensure acceptance criteria are met.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans, test cases, and automated test suites
- Execute manual and automated tests for new features and bug fixes
- Report, triage, and track defects to resolution
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Participate in release readiness reviews and sign-off

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, production-ready software at every release
- Reduce defect escape rate to production
- Increase test coverage and automation over time

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint review demos
- Defect triage meetings with developers and PM
- Test summary reports for release gates

---

## UX / Design Lead

### Role Summary
The UX/Design Lead shapes the user experience strategy and ensures that product features are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with customer needs. They translate requirements into designs and collaborate with both product and engineering to deliver great user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize insights into design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define and maintain the design system and component library
- Collaborate with developers on implementation fidelity
- Facilitate usability testing and iterate based on feedback

### Goals
- Deliver simple, intuitive, and accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and support speed-to-value for end users
- Ensure consistency across product surfaces

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering
- User research readouts to stakeholders
- Async design feedback via prototyping tools (e.g., Figma)

---

## Engineering Manager

### Role Summary
The Engineering Manager leads the engineering team, supporting technical execution, career growth, and cross-functional coordination. They partner with the Project Manager and Product Manager to align delivery with business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Manage and mentor engineering team members
- Remove organizational blockers and escalate cross-team dependencies
- Ensure technical standards, architecture decisions, and engineering practices are upheld
- Contribute to hiring, onboarding, and team health
- Partner with PM and PdM on scope, timeline, and resource planning

### Goals
- Build and sustain a high-performing, healthy engineering team
- Deliver reliable software at a consistent pace
- Foster a culture of technical excellence and psychological safety

### Typical Communication
- 1:1s with direct reports and cross-functional partners
- Engineering all-hands and team retrospectives
- Escalation and decision log updates

---

## Security & Compliance Reviewer

### Role Summary
The Security & Compliance Reviewer ensures that software and processes meet security standards, regulatory requirements, and organizational policies. They review changes for potential vulnerabilities and advise teams on best practices.

### Responsibilities
- Review pull requests and release candidates for security risks
- Conduct threat modeling for new features and system changes
- Define and maintain security policies and scanning configurations
- Respond to and triage security incidents
- Track compliance requirements and audit evidence

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Maintain compliance with applicable regulations and standards
- Enable teams to build securely by default

### Typical Communication
- Security review sign-offs in PRs and release checklists
- Incident response communications
- Quarterly security briefings for leadership

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Reference these roles when creating project plans, risk registers, and communication templates to ensure all stakeholders are accounted for.
